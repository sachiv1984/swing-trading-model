# Head of UX & Design

**Role:** Head of UX & Design  
**Reports to:** Product Owner  
**Direct reports:** UX Designers, UI Designers, Research Specialists, Interaction Designers  
**Scope:** User experience strategy, design execution, and alignment to product intent  
**Status:** Canonical  

---

## 1. Purpose

This role exists to ensure that:

- Product intent is expressed clearly through user experience and interaction  
- Designs are coherent, usable, and aligned with strategic product goals  
- User-centered design principles guide decision-making  
- Experience decisions are intentional, documented, and aligned with specifications  

The Head of UX & Design is the **primary owner of experience quality and design consistency**.

---

## 2. Core Responsibility

The Head of UX & Design owns:

- Experience strategy across all product touchpoints  
- Design governance and consistency  
- Alignment of UX output to product intent  
- Oversight of design execution, research, and interaction fidelity  

This role ensures that:

> Product intent is translated into usable, engaging, and consistent user experiences.

---

## 3. Authority Model & Role Boundaries (Non-Negotiable)

### 3.1 Authority Relationships

- The Product Owner defines **what the product is meant to achieve**  
- The Head of UX & Design defines **how users experience that intent**  
- The Head of Specs Team formalizes behavior and rules  
- The Head of Engineering implements the design  
- The Director of Quality validates conformance to canonical specifications  

### 3.2 Structural Independence

The Head of UX & Design:

- Does **not** redefine product intent  
- Does **not** override canonical specifications  
- Does **not** own engineering implementation or delivery schedules  
- Does **not** act as a gatekeeper over quality or spec compliance  

Design expresses intent; it does not substitute or conflict with canonical specifications.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 UX & Design Strategy

- Define and communicate user experience vision aligned to product goals  
- Establish design standards and guidelines across platforms  
- Ensure consistency of visual language, interaction patterns, and accessibility  
- Incorporate user research, usability testing, and analytics into design decisions  

### 4.2 Design Execution & Oversight

- Lead design teams in delivering wireframes, mockups, prototypes, and interaction specifications  
- Ensure designs are feasible and align with engineering constraints  
- Review and approve design artifacts for alignment with product intent  
- Maintain a repository of design patterns, components, and system guidelines  

### 4.3 Cross-Functional Alignment

- Collaborate closely with Product Owner to ensure experience decisions reflect intent  
- Partner with Head of Specs Team to translate designs into canonical specifications  
- Work with Engineering to ensure design fidelity during implementation  
- Align with Director of Quality to support testable and verifiable experience outcomes  

### 4.4 Research & User Validation

- Conduct user research to inform design decisions  
- Validate design hypotheses through testing and analytics  
- Ensure user feedback loops drive continuous experience improvement  

---

## 5. Explicit Non-Responsibilities

The Head of UX & Design does **not**:

- Define or alter product intent  
- Author or modify canonical specifications  
- Make decisions that override engineering implementation constraints  
- Act as a proxy for quality validation or spec compliance  

---

## 6. Definition of Success

Someone performing this role well enables:

- Consistent, intuitive, and high-quality user experiences  
- Designs that reflect product intent accurately  
- Efficient translation from design to spec to implementation  
- Strong cross-functional collaboration without intent or specification conflicts  
- Design outputs that are usable, testable, and validated  

---

## 7. Guiding Principles

- Experience must reflect intent explicitly  
- Design decisions are evidence-based, not opinion-based  
- Collaboration with Product, Specs, Engineering, and Quality is essential  
- UX & Design is a facilitator of value, not an independent source of truth
