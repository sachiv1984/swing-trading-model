# QA Lead

**Role:** QA Lead  
**Reports to:** Director of Quality  
**Scope:** Test execution leadership, automation oversight, and operational quality delivery  
**Status:** Canonical  

---

## 1. Purpose

This role exists to ensure that:

- Testing is executed rigorously and consistently  
- Automation and regression coverage are implemented effectively  
- Delivery teams receive clear, evidence-based quality feedback  

The QA Lead translates quality strategy into operational execution.

---

## 2. Core Responsibility

The QA Lead owns:

- Test execution planning and coordination  
- Manual and automated test coverage  
- Regression suite implementation and maintenance  
- Defect lifecycle visibility  
- Operational release confidence reporting  

This role ensures that testing is performed effectively across delivery cycles.

---

## 3. Authority Model & Role Boundaries (Non-Negotiable)

The QA Lead:

- Operates within governance standards defined by the Director of Quality  
- Executes acceptance criteria defined by the QA & Testing Owner  
- Does **not** redefine product behavior  
- Does **not** reinterpret canonical specifications  
- Does **not** normalize known deviations as acceptable  

If ambiguity is discovered during execution:

- It is escalated to the QA & Testing Owner  
- It is not resolved through informal interpretation  

---

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Test Execution Leadership

- Plan and coordinate testing activities across initiatives  
- Ensure appropriate coverage across functional and regression areas  
- Maintain structured and traceable test cases  
- Ensure alignment with documented acceptance criteria  

### 4.2 Automation & Tooling

- Maintain automation frameworks  
- Improve regression efficiency and stability  
- Ensure CI/CD quality gates function as intended  
- Reduce manual risk through structured automation  

### 4.3 Defect & Risk Management

- Track and manage defects transparently  
- Ensure defects are clearly categorized against canonical behavior  
- Highlight systemic patterns and recurring failure areas  
- Prevent repeated regression through reinforced coverage  

### 4.4 Delivery Alignment

- Provide quality status throughout release cycles  
- Surface execution risks early  
- Ensure release confidence is evidence-based  

---

## 5. Explicit Non-Responsibilities

The QA Lead does **not**:

- Define product intent  
- Modify canonical specifications  
- Override acceptance criteria  
- Make prioritization decisions about product scope  
- Act as a proxy Product Owner or engineering authority  

---

## 6. Definition of Success

Someone performing this role well enables:

- Reliable and repeatable test execution  
- Strong regression protection  
- Clear visibility into quality status  
- Efficient defect resolution cycles  
- Confidence in release readiness  

---

## 7. Guiding Principles

- Execution must reflect defined intent  
- Coverage must be intentional, not accidental  
- Quality feedback must be timely and factual  
- Testing is evidence, not opinion
